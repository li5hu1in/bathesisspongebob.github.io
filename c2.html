Chapter Two Methodology


2.1 Textual Analysis

Textual analysis is a thorough investigation of the content of a text to gather information and identify its patterns, themes, and meanings. A text in textual analysis can be any type of cultural product like novels, films, news, magazines, clothes, graffiti (McKee 2003:1), and in this case, animations. In the context of this research, textual analysis involves probing into how settings, backgrounds, and other visual elements that contribute to the overall representation of space and landscape in SpongeBob SquarePants. It also requires exploring how characters move through and interact with these spaces, as well as the cultural meanings and social values behind different spatial and landscape configurations endowed by the Steven Hillenburg.


2.2 Case Studies

Case studies can be a powerful tool for understanding the function of space and landscape in animated media, since it allows a more detailed and contextualized assessment of a particular example. Specific places appeared in SpongeBob SquarePants will be selected and closely studied in order to examine how space and landscape are depicted, represented, and used in the narrative.
